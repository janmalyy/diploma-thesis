**Getting Started**

To generate a summary, provide the variant details in the input form:

- Level: Select the coordinate system (Protein, Transcript, Genome, dbSNP, or COSMIC).
- Gene: Enter the official gene symbol (e.g., BRCA1 or NOP10). This field is hidden for dbSNP and COSMIC searches.
- Change/ID: Enter the specific alteration (e.g., V600E, c.34G>C) or the reference ID (e.g., rs113488022).

**Understanding the Results**
- Narrative Summary: A high-level overview of the variant’s functional impact and clinical relevance found in the literature with links to the articles.
- Pathogenicity & Confidence: The tool assigns a classification (e.g., Pathogenic, Likely Pathogenic, Benign) and a confidence level (High, Moderate, Low) based on the strength of extracted evidence.
- Evidence Counts: WIP
- Detailed Evidence by Article: The evidence contains the quoted texts from the article, the description of the quote, type of the evidence, and the claim towards the pathogenicity. The evidences are grouped by article

**Navigation Tips**
- Interactive Citations: Click on PMC or PMID numbers within the narrative summary to open a detailed modal showing the specific quotes and claims extracted from that article.
- Article Cards: Scroll to the bottom to view "Detailed Evidence by Article." You can navigate between these cards using the arrow keys on your keyboard.
- Copying Data: Use the "Copy" buttons to quickly export the narrative summary or specific evidence points to your clipboard.
- Stopping Generation: If the process takes too long, you can use the "Stop Generation" button or press the Escape key to cancel the request.
