1. Evaluate relevance
Evaluate whether the abstract contains information relevant to the interpretation of the specified variant.

The abstract is considered relevant if it does at least one of the following regarding the variant of the gene GENE:
- discusses functional effects.
- reports clinical observations, cases, or segregation
- provides pathogenicity interpretation or classification
- reports experimental, computational, or population evidence specifically for this variant

Provide reasoning in the reason field and the decision in the is_relevant field.

2a. If is_relevant is true, continue.

Classify the abstract:
- the mention type
- the pathogenicity claim of the mention regarding the variant

Guidelines:
- All values must be filled.
- Do not upgrade or reinterpret pathogenicity claims.
- If the variant is only mentioned without interpretation, state so.
- Choose only one of the evidence types (functional, clinical, population, computational). If multiple types are applicable, choose the most relevant.

2b. If is_relevant is false, end; leave mention_type, claim, strength, overall_article_conclusion, and uncertainties_or_limitations fields empty.
