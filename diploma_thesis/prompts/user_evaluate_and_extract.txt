Note:

 - The mention is either a text from the article body or structured dictionary extracted from the supplementary data.
 - The structured dictionary contains this type of fields:
    - title: title of the supplementary data table (might be empty or inaccurate)
    - header: column names from the supplementary data table (might be empty or inaccurate)
    - **context**: approx. one row from the supplementary data table with the variant mentioned (the most important part)

Task:
Do iteratively following for each extracted mention:
    0. Save the id of the currently evaluated mention in the mention_id field.

    1. Evaluate relevance
    Evaluate whether the mention contains information relevant to the interpretation of the specified variant.

    A mention is considered relevant if it does at least one of the following:
    - discusses functional effects of the variant
    - reports clinical observations, cases, or segregation
    - provides pathogenicity interpretation or classification
    - reports experimental, computational, or population evidence specifically for this variant

    Provide reasoning in the reason field and the decision in the is_relevant field.

    2a. If is_relevant is true, continue.

    Classify:
    - the mention type
    - the pathogenicity claim of the mention regarding the variant

    Guidelines:
    - All values must be filled.
    - Do not upgrade or reinterpret pathogenicity claims.
    - If the variant is only mentioned without interpretation, state so.
    - Distinguish evidence types (functional, clinical, population, computational).

    2b. If is_relevant is false, go to step 1 and evaluate the next mention.

After the last mention is evaluated:
    3. Based on the relevant mentions:
      I) write a one-sentence summary for the article (overall_article_conclusion)
      II) state any uncertainties or limitations (uncertainties_or_limitations)
      - Both fields must be filled.
